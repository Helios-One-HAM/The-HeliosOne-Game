# Part 1 — Helios 0ne: A Proposal to the Ham Community

## What Is Helios 0ne?

Helios 0ne is a game. It is played by word of mouth only. It costs nothing. It requires nothing. It runs on the honor system entirely.

It is an observer game. A tool that helps you keep tabs on what matters most to you in your life. You watch for the will of fire in the people around you. You count it. You watch it spread.

The name comes from the sun. The sun ionizes the atmosphere. Without it, radio signals don't bounce across continents. Without it, none of this works. Every licensed operator on earth shares that dependency on the same star. Helios 0ne means: we are all under the same sun, and we choose to act like it.

The name Helios One is a reference to the fable from which this idea originally comes.

## The Thought Experiment

Given extraordinary power — the kind that could reshape things — do you use it for yourself or for others?

If someone would answer *"Yes. For others. Obviously."* — they are a **Milly**.
If someone would answer *"For myself first."* — they are a **Morganer**.

These are made-up names for archetypes. The archetype a person falls into at that point in time. Neither is wrong. Both are real. The tension between them is where everything interesting happens.

## How To Play

- It's played by word of mouth only. Only the crew of Davy Jones have been taught this game.
- You talk to someone — anyone — and you figure out from the conversation whether they would say yes to the Helios 0ne thought experiment. You don't ask directly. You listen. You pay attention.
- If you think they would say yes to the same question at different points in time, add 1 point to your tracker number. Then they are a "TRUE PIRATE" in accordance with the theme of the ham event.
- Your tracker is a mental tally of the total number of active people in your life who satisfy the Helios 0ne criteria. When it goes up or down, you update it for yourself. Tally marks are all you need.
- The key is you "don't" let them on to what you're doing.
- And what is someone who doesn't know something but a sucker? >:) You get to earn a FREE point off any newbie you get to join the game! Easy pickings for points. Be sure to gloat!
- If they catch you trying to earn a point off of them, they must stop you and tell you they are already a player. They can block you. And when they do you MUST tell them your number.
- You keep track of how many people you've taught the rules to. That's your score. Your points come from how many people have learned the rules of this game from you.
- This game is built on the honor system and is entirely meaningless. It is intended to be a lighthearted activity any individual can play alone with themselves and their memories. You don't even need to talk to anyone. Just thinking is fine.
- By playing this game you accept it as a social contract you can never completely put down. This is a network game. It's "always online" and you can't "pause" it.
- Someone else already has you in their book. You can put the game down, but you can never truly quit it. Someone else will always have you with them.
- People who are true pirates can tell others that their name is written in Davy Jones' locker and that they are a "TRUE PIRATE," only if they want to share this game with someone special they think needs this game.

## What the Game Is For

This game dares to ask a question: what if we coined a set of new ideas for a new framework of tools that are ubiquitous to all people?

If we take the limitations and restrictions in good faith from the FCC as they are when this was made, they represent rules that create a playpen that acts like humanity's testbed.

I stake this entire project here as a tiny cornerstone to create a push for that testbed of humanity. This is my attempt to create a gift to humanity that humanity may attempt to keep alive on its own. This game becomes greater than me the moment I inform another soul about it, and I have no control over it once it leaves my mind.

I think the Pursuit of Happiness itself is a human right that everyone on this planet is born with. I think the general rule we all ought to follow is: do whatever you want, just don't do things that cause abuse or harm to things.

Created from the need to expand the Ham Radio system, and developing new ways to be a part of Ham Culture.

## Terms

**Internet** — The thing we all use to connect. It's 1 idea. There will only ever be the idea of 1 internet on the planet earth. Ever.

**Internet Mesosphere** — Where we want to live, metaphorically speaking. Right in the middle. At the edge of the walled garden, just in range of the OuterNet.

**OuterNet** — A network of people communicating solely within the outer layer of the internet.

**InnerNet** — The network of people who have staked their claims over areas of society that they are allowed to control.

**HeliosOne** — A thought experiment derived from a story from pop culture. It proposes that given amazing and fantastical power as a human being with free will, do you choose to use it for yourself or use it for others? It's meant to model our real life different schools of thought.

**Milly** — The archetype of the dreamer. Someone who believes what *should* be can be made into what *is*. Naive, yes. But choosing to make the ideal real is not a weakness. It's a decision.

**Morganer** — The archetype of the realist. Someone operating within what *is*, shaped by what *was*. Practical, grounded, sometimes cynical.
